Fin Flutter Research Project

FURI


The FURI funded project, officially titled "Fin Flutter Study: The Effects of Fin Geometry and Motor Design on a Sounding Rocket", is a project I created with the help of My mentors Daniel White and Lauren Brunacini. It began with the intent of building a sounding rocket equipped with electronics capable of measuring the flutter in fins. The goal was to study what fin profiles reduced the effect of flutter the most. While analyzing costs of the project, it was found that the cost of purchasing enough motors to gain sufficient data far exceed the alloted funding given by FURI. So, instead of cancelling or changing the project, myself and ther other leaders of my club Daedalus Astronautics saw an opportunity. Our Solid Propellant team was in the process of deciding on a project for the 2016-2017 school year to focus on, and I put forward the idea of the team working towards the development of flight capable motors.
